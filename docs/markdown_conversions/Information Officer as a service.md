# Information Officer as a service

What is the Protection Personal Information Act designed to do?

The Protection of Personal Information Act (Act 4 of 2013) is there to protect the personal information of individuals and juristic entities and ensure that companies who store and process that information do so within the framework of the Law.

The Protection of Personal Information Act is there to promote the protection of personal information processed by public and private bodies.

It has been created to ensure a minimum standard is met when processing personal information.

The act details the establishment of an Information Regulator, who will be expected to exercise certain powers to ensure the rights of South Africans, relating to their personal information and how it can be used;

The organization’s designated Information Officer will be required to register with the Information Regulator as the point of contact for the business and be the defined resource that is responsible for driving the organization’s compliance towards the Act.

How will this help Business?

The POPI act will bring South Africa in line with international standards, when it comes to the protection of personal information. This will change the way in which business and government treat and handle personal information.

Through change, new business opportunities can be created; security process can be refined and streamlined thus ensuring more efficient entities that are able to

implement the correct controls and mitigate risk relating to Information Security.

How will this help Individuals?

POPI is about ensuring that individuals and juristic entities enjoy the protection of their personal information. All South Africans have the right to privacy. This act is now the enforcement of that right as set out in the constitution.

Addressing the need for an Information Officer

With the advent of the Protection of Personal Information Act, Securepath Consulting has designed an outsource capability that allows organizations to leverage the expertise of the experienced resources at Securepath Consulting. This service will assist organisations on the road to achieving compliance in what are challenging timeframes set out by the legislation.

Deliverables of the Information Officer as a service

The outsourced Information officer will implement the Data Protection framework with a reporting line to the Directors and act as Chair/Secretary to the POPI Working Group ensuring that a representative of each department is a member of the group. The group will be trained on data protection issues and will monitor and share information to ensure that practices across the organisation are consistently communicated.

Further responsibilities of the Data Protection officer will be to keep up to date the Data Protection policies and procedures including the breach management policy and to disseminate new rules/regulations on the POPI Act to staff.

Analyse the types of breaches of the POPI Act within the organisation and create and maintain relevant procedures for staff as well as provide advice on projects, programmes and data sharing.

They will also advise directorate data controllers on the POPI Act including on the Privacy and Electronic Communications regulations where appropriate and their implementation within each department’s area of responsibility as well as advise on all elements of processing personal data abroad and on the requirements and implications of local Data Protection laws.

Dealing with the Information Regulator’s complaints as well as helping with the resolution of complaints from the public and staff.

The Data Protection officer will also conduct frequent audits of data for compliance and maintain an internal register of Personal Data Processing projects and systems.

The first steps to becoming POPI compliant

The first step working towards compliance with the Act is in ensuring that the organisation establishes a data protection officer function either internally through the appointment of a dedicated resource or by taking advantage of Securepath Consulting’s information Officer as a service.
